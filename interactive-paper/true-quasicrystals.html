Quasicrystal Generators

Interactive tools for generating aperiodic structures with long-range order

About Quasicrystals

Quasicrystals are ordered structures that lack translational periodicity. Unlike regular crystals, they never repeat exactly, yet they exhibit long-range order and produce sharp diffraction peaks. The generators below create two types of quasicrystalline patterns:

  • Penrose tilings produce discrete aperiodic arrangements of tiles
  • Wave interference patterns create continuous density fields with quasiperiodic structure

Method A: Penrose Tiling (P3 Rhombus Tiling)

The Penrose tiling uses an inflation algorithm on Robinson triangles. Each generation subdivides triangles according to rules involving the golden ratio. The resulting pattern has 5-fold rotational symmetry but no translational symmetry.

5

Properties of Penrose Tilings

  • Aperiodic: no translational symmetry exists
  • 5-fold rotational symmetry at the center
  • Self-similar under scaling by the golden ratio
  • Composed of two rhombus types (thick and thin)

Method B: Wave Interference with Incommensurate Frequencies

This method superimposes plane waves with carefully chosen wave vectors. Using two sets of waves whose wavelengths are in the golden ratio ensures incommensurability, producing a continuous density field with quasicrystalline order.

The density field is given by the sum of cosine waves with incommensurate frequencies.

Properties of Wave Quasicrystals

  • Aperiodic due to incommensurate wavelengths
  • Produces sharp Bragg peaks in the Fourier transform
  • Continuous density field rather than discrete tiles
  • Models soft matter quasicrystals

Comparison of Methods

Penrose Tiling

  • Discrete tile arrangement
  • Based on subdivision rules
  • Fixed 5-fold symmetry
  • Models atomic quasicrystals

Wave Interference

  • Continuous density field
  • Based on wave superposition
  • Adjustable n-fold symmetry
  • Models soft matter systems
